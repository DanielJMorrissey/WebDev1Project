Ole Gunnar Solskjaer- Current Manager

Another Brief Biography

The Time Ole was a Player

Ole made his debut at the ripe old age of 17 for a Norwegian club called Clausenengen in 1990. He performed well for Clausenengen where he averaged more than a goal per game and aided in their promotion from the Third Division to the Second Division. Ole stayed at Clausenengen until late 1994 where he then joined Molde.

At Molde, Ole scored a total of 41 goals in 54 appearances. It was here that Ole had his first taste of European football, playing in the qualifying round of the UEFA Cup Winners Cup. Ole stayed at Molde for a total of 2 years before joining the best club in the world, Manchester United in 1996.

Ole was a surprise to many people as very few people knew of Ole outside of Norway and expected him to be a back up to Eric Cantona. However, Ole and Ferguson proved that Ole would be very important to the club scoring 18 goals in 33 appearances in his first season and out of those 33 appearances, 25 were games he started. It was this season he earned the nickname 'Baby-faced Assassin' due to his youthful looks and clinical finishing.

Ole continued to score for Manchester United and scored the winning goal in the Champions League final against Bayern Munich in 1999, the Treble winning season. However by the turn of the millennium, opportunities became limited for Ole as the likes of Ruud Van Nistelrooy joined Manchester United and then followed by seasons plagued with injuries. It wasn't until August 2006 that he made a proper return to the Premier League. This season proved to be his last season, not only for Manchester United but also for his playing career.

Ole's Time as a Manager

Ole first started off coaching the Manchester United Reserves in May 2008. He won the 2007-08 Lancashire Senior Cup by defeating the Liverpool Reserves 3-2. A couple of years later, Ole won the Manchester Senior Cup. During his time with Manchester United Reserves, he was offered to take over as manager for the Norwegian National Team but he declined as he believed he was not ready for such an undertaking. Ole resigned as manager of the Reserves in December of 2010 and became manager of Molde.

At Molde, it took 3 games for Ole to get his first win and went on to win the Tippeligaen (the Norwegian equivalent of the Premier League) in his first season as manager. Molde won the Tippeligaen twice under Ole and the Norwegian Football Cup once. In January of 2014, Ole was announced as the manager of Cardiff City. Even though Ole won his first game against Newcastle, Cardiff struggled to get points in the Premier League, winning a total of 30 points and Cardiff City finished 20th in the Premier League. Ole started the next season at Cardiff poorly in the Championship where he left in September and returned to Molde.

Upon his return to Molde, the clubs performances improved, coming from a mid-table team to challenging for the title. But Molde never won the League during Ole's second spell as manager there. After Manchester United sacked Mourinho, Ole initially took charge of Manchester United as caretaker manager. Ole's first few months as caretaker manager was incredible. Manchester United went on an unbeaten run until the middle of February 2019, where they lost 2-0 to Paris Saint-Germain (aka PSG) at home in the first leg of the Champions League Round of 16 tie but went on to knock out PSG i the second leg. In March, Ole became the permanent manager signing a three year contract. Since then, the clubs form has gone downhill and the 2019/2020 season is looking to become a poor season but there is some hope in the works as Manchester United are still in a period of transition and if given enough time from the idiotic business men in charge, Manchester United may get back to winning ways.
